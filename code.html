CODE OF CONDUCT

 

Preamble

This is the Personal Code of Conduct that defines the obligations that we must uphold with respect to our clients, peers, the community, the environment, and the profession as a whole. We strive to be ethical in all we do, and this code is to formally document the standards to which we are to be held.

The Profession

  • We will not engage in speculative work, as we feel that the practice devalues the work of designers, in particular those new to the field.

  • Work will be done at a fair market value, and we will not knowingly attempt to undercut, or devalue similar work being done.

The Client

  • We will always work with the best interests of our clients in mind.

  • We will only engage in work previously agreed upon in writing that specifies the scope, deliverables, compensation, and any other obligations for any involved parties.

  • We will not mislead clients as to the work that is possible relative to the budget, or technological limitations.

  • Work that is done for clients is owned by the clients, and as such that work, including works in progress may only be shared with permission from the client.

  • We will not engage in work with clients that may be seen to cause a conflict of interest.

  • We will not disclose any client information to any outside parties.

  • We will inform clients if a request would require violation of This code, and work to negotiate possible alternatives, or refuse the requested work.

The Community

  • We will not accept any work that has the purpose of infringing upon the rights or freedoms of either a group, or individual.

  • We will not accept work that we feel is attempting to exploit a group, or individual.

  • We will not accept work that seeks to exploit any protected natural resources or land.

Compensation

  • We will not take part in unpaid projects, with the exception of approved work for a registered charity organization, or family members.

  • We will not take part in the judging of speculative work for commercial purposes.

  • All work will be agreed upon in writing, including compensation, by all relevant parties before it is done.

Intellectual Property and Copyright

  • We will use no copyrighted content without obtaining permission or license to use said content.

  • While the client retians ownership of the work done, we will request that design credit be included discretely on work whenever possible.

  • We will inform the client if we discover any instances of possible copyright infringement we believe them to be unwaware of.
